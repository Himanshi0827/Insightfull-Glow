Hair Assessment Test

Note

Start with freshly washed hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to ensure your hair is clean and free from product buildup.

Checking Your Hair Texture Type

 Examine your hair strands. Run your fingers along a few strands of hair, paying attention to their texture.

Fine Hair

If your hair strands are very thin and delicate, you likely have fine hair.

Fine to Normal Hair

If your hair strands are fine but not extremely thin, you likely have fine to normal hair.

Medium Hair

If your hair strands have a medium thickness and feel neither very thin nor very coarse, you likely have medium hair.

Coarse Hair

If your hair strands are thick and feel rough or coarse, you likely have coarse hair.





Checking Your Hair Shine & Condition

Hair Shine and Condition: Examine your hair's natural shine and how it feels.

Oily Hair

If your hair feels greasy and looks shiny shortly after washing, you likely have oily hair.

Normal to Oily Hair

If your roots are oily but the rest of your hair feels normal, you likely have normal to oily hair.

Normal to Dry Hair

If your roots are oily, but your hair feels dry or rough at the ends, you likely have normal to slightly dry hair.

Dry Hair

If your hair feels dry, brittle, and lacks shine, you likely have dry hair.





Checking Your Scalp Condition

 Pay attention to your scalp's condition.

Oily Scalp

If your scalp is consistently oily and prone to dandruff, you likely have an oily scalp.

Oily T-Zone Scalp

If your scalp is oily primarily in the T-zone (forehead area), you likely have an oily T-zone scalp.

Normal Scalp

If your scalp feels comfortable with occasional dryness or flaking, you likely have a normal scalp.

Dry Scalp

If your scalp is dry, itchy, or prone to tightness, you likely have a dry scalp.

Hair Care Tips
Wash your hair regularly using a mild shampoo suitable for your hair type.
Condition your hair after every wash to keep it soft and manageable.
Avoid hot water for hair washing as it can strip away natural oils. Use lukewarm or cool water instead.
Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle wet hair gently to prevent breakage.
Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy hair growth.
Avoid excessive use of heating tools like hairdryers, straighteners, and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
Protect your hair from the sun's harmful UV rays by wearing a hat or using hair products with UV protection.
Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, causing breakage and hair loss.
Eat a balanced diet rich in nutrients, including vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair.
Stay hydrated by drinking enough water, as dehydration can affect your hair's health.
Consider using hair masks and treatments to nourish and repair your hair, especially if it's damaged.
